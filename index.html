02 — Macro View

Aviation as the
Unseen Engine

$8T

03 — Micro View

Flying as a
Marker of Privilege

1%
The Rule
Just 1% of the world generates 50% of all commercial aviation emissions. Hypermobility is hyper-concentrated.
70%
Income Link
In the US, the top 20% of earners take 70% of all flights. Flight frequency is a near-perfect proxy for wealth.
75%
Profit from 6%
Premium passengers — only 6% of travellers — generate 75% of airline profits. The model is built for the affluent.

Modern airline economics are a mirror of inequality: designed for, funded by, and optimised around the world's wealthiest passengers.

05 — Synthesis

A Dual-Track
Future

The Verdict
Air travel is a systemic macroeconomic necessity and an individual microeconomic luxury — simultaneously. Any single label is a distortion of reality.
Modal Shift to Rail Carbon Pricing Private Jet Caps UNHAS Funding SAF Investment
Policy Dual-Track
🛡
Subsidise the Necessity
Protect air cargo lifelines, humanitarian airlifts (UNHAS), and remote community connectivity. These are infrastructure — not amenities.
⚖
Strictly Regulate the Luxury
Tax hypermobility, ban or cap private jets, and mandate high-speed rail for short-haul routes of 200–500 miles where greener options exist.
High-speed rail can replace the "luxury of time" for short-haul flights. The plane is not always the answer — only where no greener alternative exists should aviation remain the default choice.
